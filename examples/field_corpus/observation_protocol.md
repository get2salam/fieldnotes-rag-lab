# Observation and Recording Protocol — Field Station Alpha

**Version:** 3.0  
**Applies to:** All field staff conducting wildlife, vegetation, and water quality surveys  

---

## Principles of Field Recording

Good field records are accurate, precise, legible, and dated. They are made at the time of observation — never reconstructed from memory hours later. A note that says "saw a duck" is nearly useless; "one adult male Common Merganser (Mergus merganser), swimming north in slow pool at R-4-07, 07:43, in full eclipse plumage" is a data record.

The goal is reproducibility: another observer, reading your notes, should be able to understand exactly what you saw, where, when, and under what conditions.

## Standard Data Fields

All observations must include:

| Field | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Date | YYYY-MM-DD | 2024-03-12 |
| Time | HH:MM local | 18:45 |
| Observer(s) | Initials | FSA / RK |
| Location | Survey marker | R-4-07 |
| GPS (optional) | Decimal degrees | 49.4521 N, 121.8834 W |
| Species | Full common name | American Dipper |
| Scientific name | Genus species | Cinclus mexicanus |
| Count | Exact or estimated | 1 adult |
| Sex/Age | M/F/U, Ad/Imm | 1 adult, sex unknown |
| Behaviour | Activity at observation | Foraging, submerged in riffle |
| Microhabitat | Specific location | Fast riffle below cascade, on emergent cobble |
| Confidence | High/Medium/Low | High |
| Weather summary | See Weather Log | Clear, SW 5 kph, 11°C |
| Notes | Any notable detail | Tail bob rate approx 2/sec; no banding |

## Recording Dusk Observations

Dusk surveys (civil twilight to nautical twilight) require particular care because light conditions change rapidly and identification certainty may vary through the session.

For dusk observations, additionally record:
- **Light phase:** Civil twilight (sun 0–6° below horizon), nautical twilight (6–12°), or darkness
- **Visibility distance:** Estimated maximum reliable detection range in metres at time of observation
- **Illumination source:** Fading sky, moon (if present and phase), artificial light if any
- **ID method:** Visual (describe light level), auditory (call, song, wing noise), structural (silhouette)

For each dusk survey, note the transition times:
- Sunset (civil twilight begins)
- End of civil twilight (30 min after sunset approximately)
- End of nautical twilight (approx 60 min after sunset)

Bird activity patterns at dusk:
- Raptors active through civil twilight; most retire after
- Owls begin calling at or after end of civil twilight
- Passerines typically cease foraging at end of civil twilight
- Bats begin foraging at end of civil twilight
- Nightjars (e.g. Common Nighthawk) active from nautical twilight

## Streamside Observation Protocols

When recording observations at or near running water:

### Positioning
- Approach from downstream to avoid disturbing water surface
- Crouch or sit to reduce silhouette against sky
- Maintain at least 10 m from bank edge for sensitive habitats (active redds, nesting banks)
- Use natural cover where available; avoid breaking skyline

### Light and Glare
- Position sun behind you when possible for visibility into water
- Polarised sunglasses improve subsurface visibility by 70–80%
- At dusk, use binoculars even at close range — eyes adjust slower than optics

### Recording at Water
- Record current velocity qualitatively: still, slow (<0.3 m/s), moderate (0.3–1 m/s), fast (>1 m/s)
- Note whether animal was at surface, subsurface, on bank, or in riparian vegetation
- For aquatic species: note water depth class — shallow (<0.3 m), mid (0.3–1 m), deep (>1 m)

## Photographic Documentation

Photographs supplement but do not replace written records. For each photograph:
- Record time (matches camera EXIF if clock is set correctly)
- Note subject species and behaviour being photographed
- Record whether photograph is ID-quality or documentation-only
- Upload to project shared drive within 48 hours with filename: `YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS_species_location.jpg`

Camera flash is prohibited near nesting birds and amphibian breeding sites.

## Data Entry and Submission

Field notes must be transferred to the project database within 5 working days of each survey. Use the standardised data entry spreadsheet (Field_Data_v3.xlsx) — do not create your own format.

Check for the following before submission:
- All required fields completed
- Confidence level assigned
- Weather data linked to survey
- GPS or location code present
- Any exceptional records flagged with *** and email notification to project coordinator

Retain original field notebooks. They are primary records and may be required for permit audits.
