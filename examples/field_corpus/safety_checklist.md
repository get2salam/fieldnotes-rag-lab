# Field Safety Checklist — Ridgeline Creek Survey Operations

**Version:** 2.1  
**Applies to:** All personnel conducting surveys in R-4 through R-7 sector  
**Review date:** 2024-01-15  

---

## Pre-Departure Checklist

Complete this checklist before leaving the trailhead for any survey lasting more than 2 hours.

### Communication
- [ ] File a trip plan with the station coordinator (location, route, expected return)
- [ ] Verify satellite communicator is charged and functioning (test send)
- [ ] Confirm cell coverage map for the route (coverage ends at approximately R-5-04)
- [ ] Identify emergency contact number and share with a non-field contact

### Equipment
- [ ] First aid kit: inspect for completeness, check expiry dates on medications
- [ ] Navigation: map (waterproof, 1:25000), compass, GPS device with charged batteries
- [ ] Emergency shelter: bivy or space blanket in pack
- [ ] Water: minimum 2 L per person plus purification tablets or filter
- [ ] Food: minimum 200 kcal per hour of planned survey plus 500 kcal emergency reserve
- [ ] Headlamp with fresh batteries (mandatory for dusk surveys)
- [ ] High-visibility vest or jacket

### Clothing
- [ ] Moisture-wicking base layer (no cotton)
- [ ] Insulating mid-layer (fleece or down, packed even in warm weather)
- [ ] Waterproof outer shell
- [ ] Waterproof boots with ankle support
- [ ] Hat and gloves (packed; mandatory below 8°C)

## Hazard Awareness

### Stream Crossing Hazard

Stream crossings are the highest-risk activity in this survey sector. Review Weather Observation Log for current gauge reading before any crossing.

Crossing rules:
1. Never cross alone — buddy system mandatory
2. Undo hip belt and loosen shoulder straps before entering water
3. Use a stout pole on the upstream side for stability
4. Cross at designated fords only (marked on sector map)
5. If swept off feet: roll onto back, feet downstream, do not fight current; work toward bank

### Wildlife Hazard

Black bear (Ursus americanus) activity confirmed in sector R-6. Report sightings immediately. Carry bear spray; know how to use it.

Do not approach nesting raptors within 150 m during breeding season (March–July). Osprey and Cooper's Hawk are known nesters in this sector.

Stinging nettles are widespread near R-5-03 ford; wear long trousers and gloves when sampling in dense vegetation.

Western Toad (Anaxyrus boreas) breeding aggregations occur at R-7-pool in April–May. Avoid walking through shallow water at these times; toad eggs and tadpoles are sensitive to mechanical disturbance.

### Terrain Hazard

Steep bank sections are present at R-4-cascade and R-6-gorge. Do not approach bank edge within 1 m without checking stability. Undercut banks are not always visible from above.

Wet roots and mossy logs in shaded sections are extremely slippery. Test footing before committing weight.

## Incident Response

### Minor Injury (cuts, sprains, minor hypothermia)
1. Stop survey; assess and treat in the field
2. Note time and location of incident
3. Decide whether to continue or return to trailhead
4. Document incident in field log
5. Report to coordinator on return

### Major Injury or Illness
1. Activate satellite communicator — send position and nature of emergency
2. Stay with casualty; do not leave unless to get help in a life-threatening situation
3. Administer first aid within your training level
4. Keep casualty warm and sheltered
5. Await rescue; conserve phone/radio battery

### Lost or Overdue
If a team is overdue by more than 2 hours from the filed return time:
1. Coordinator initiates welfare check by phone/satellite
2. If no contact in 30 minutes: notify search and rescue
3. Teams in the field should stop and shelter in place if lost; do not wander

|---|---|
| Trailhead cache box | Emergency bivouac, flare kit, written emergency contacts |

## Sign-Off

All personnel must confirm they have read this checklist and understand the protocols before the first survey of each season. Record name, date, and signature in the operations log at the trailhead cache box.

Safety briefing reminder: wilderness first aid and swift water awareness training is **mandatory** for stream-wading surveys. Non-trained personnel must remain on bank at all crossing sites.
