## Weather Impact on Field Observations

### Temperature Effects on Wildlife Activity

Cold mornings (below 5°C) suppress diurnal reptile activity entirely. Passerine foraging activity is elevated in the first two hours after dawn following a cold night — birds compensate for overnight energy deficit.

Water temperature lagged air temperature by approximately 2 days following the March 11 rain event. Stream temperature at R-4 dropped 1.8°C after the influx of snowmelt; monitor for fish stress if sustained below 2°C.

### Wind Effects

Wind speed above 20 kph reduces auditory detection probability for bird surveys by approximately 30–50%. Schedule call playback surveys on calm mornings. Flag survey records with wind speed; high-wind records have lower detection reliability.

Above 30 kph: canopy movement increases false-positive rate for small bird detection by eye. Prioritise auditory methods.

### Precipitation Effects

Light rain (< 2 mm/h): most mammals and birds continue activity; waterfowl often increases. Continue survey with waterproof notes.

Moderate rain (2–10 mm/h): passerine activity suppressed. Defer point-count surveys. Opportunistic observations only.

Heavy rain (> 10 mm/h): suspend all surveys. Record time and return to shelter. Stream crossing risk elevated.

Post-rain: macroinvertebrate emergence often peaks 2–6 hours after rain ceases. Plan aquatic insect surveys accordingly.

## Flood Risk and Stream Crossing Safety

Monitor gauge at R-4 before any stream crossing in sectors R-5 to R-7.

| Gauge Reading | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| < +0.10 m | Normal | Cross at designated ford |
| +0.10–0.25 m | Elevated | Use trekking poles; extra caution |
| +0.25–0.40 m | Caution | Experienced staff only; buddy system |
| > +0.40 m | Dangerous | No crossing; use alternate route via bridge |

After heavy rain (>15 mm in 12 hours), assume gauge is at Caution level even without direct reading. Wait at least 6 hours post-precipitation peak before crossing.

## Recording Weather at Each Observation Point

Minimum weather data to log with any wildlife or vegetation observation:
- Sky cover: clear, partly cloudy (1–5 oktas), mostly cloudy (6–8 oktas), overcast
- Wind: Beaufort scale class (0=calm, 1–3=light, 4–5=moderate, 6+=strong)
- Precipitation: none, trace, light, moderate, heavy
- Temperature: nearest degree Celsius
- Time since last rain (relevant for soil and stream condition)

Weather recorded at the time of observation, not interpolated from earlier readings.
