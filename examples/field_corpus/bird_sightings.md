# Bird Sightings Log — Ridgeline Creek Trail

**Location:** Ridgeline Creek, sector R-4  
**Habitat type:** Riparian / mixed conifer  
**Observer:** Field Station Alpha  

---

## 2024-03-12 — Dusk Survey

Time: 18:45–19:30 local.
Weather: Clear, wind SW 5–8 kph, temperature 11°C.

Observed an American Dipper (Cinclus mexicanus) foraging along the fast-moving riffle at the base of the cascade. The bird repeatedly submerged into the current for 3–5 seconds, emerging with invertebrate prey. Characteristic tail-bobbing behaviour confirmed ID. No banding marks visible.

Field marks to record for streamside passerines at dusk:
- Note beak shape (straight vs curved, stout vs slender)
- Tail posture: cocked, drooping, or level
- Wing pattern when flushed: bars, patches, or plain
- Stance: upright vs horizontal
- Foraging substrate: rock surface, water surface, emergent vegetation, open water

A Belted Kingfisher (Megaceryle alcyon) was heard calling from downstream before dusk. The rattling call was unmistakable. Not visually confirmed at this location.

Two Common Mergansers (Mergus merganser) rafted in the slow pool above the riffle. Adults, male–female pair. Arrived at 19:10 and remained through observation end.

## 2024-03-19 — Morning Survey

Time: 06:15–07:45 local.
Weather: Partly cloudy, calm, temperature 8°C.

Spotted a pair of Osprey (Pandion haliaetus) circling over the wider pool downstream of marker R-4-07. One individual made three dive attempts, successful on the third. Fish species not identified from distance but appeared to be a salmonid, approximately 25 cm.

Yellow Warbler (Setophaga petechia) detected at the willow fringe along the east bank — first spring arrival for this survey block. Song only, no visual confirmation.

Oregon Junco (Junco hyemalis oreganus) flock of approximately 15 birds feeding on the gravel bar, seeds from dried sedge heads. Departed when the Osprey circled low.

## 2024-04-02 — Breeding Season Check

Time: 07:00–09:00 local.
Weather: Overcast, 14°C, light rain beginning at 08:30.

Dipper nest confirmed at the culvert under the forest road at R-4 marker. Nest is moss-domed, attached to the concrete lip above the water line. One adult entering and exiting — incubation likely underway. Do not approach within 15 m; avoid flash photography.

Harlequin Duck (Histrionicus histrionicus) pair observed in turbulent chute at R-4-03. Male in full breeding plumage — slate-blue, chestnut flanks, white facial patches. Highly unusual occurrence for this drainage; verify with regional coordinator before submitting to database.

## Observation Protocol for Dusk Streamside Surveys

After observing a streamside bird at dusk, the following data must be recorded:
1. Species (confirmed ID or best estimate with uncertainty noted)
2. Sex and age class if determinable
3. Exact microhabitat: perched on emergent rock, hovering over riffle, foraging along bank, in open water
4. Behavior at time of observation: foraging, calling, resting, flying
5. Light conditions: full daylight, civil twilight, nautical twilight, darkness
6. Estimated visibility distance in metres
7. Any vocalisations: describe or phonetically transcribe
8. Duration of observation
9. GPS coordinates or reference to nearest survey marker
10. Any other species present in the same frame

Record all data in the field notebook immediately; do not rely on memory. Photograph if possible without disturbing the bird.
