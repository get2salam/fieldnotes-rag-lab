QUERY: What should I record after seeing a streamside bird at dusk?

ANSWER:
For dusk observations, additionally record: light phase (civil twilight, nautical
twilight, or darkness), visibility distance in metres, illumination source, and
identification method (visual, auditory, or structural silhouette). For each dusk
survey note the transition times — sunset, end of civil twilight, and end of
nautical twilight [1]. At the observation point, approach from downstream to avoid
disturbing the water surface. Crouch or sit to reduce silhouette against the sky.
Maintain at least 10 m from bank edge for sensitive habitats [2]. Record the
observation immediately; note species, sex, age class, exact microhabitat, and
the bird's behaviour at time of observation [3].

QUERY: What safety precautions should I take before a stream crossing?

ANSWER:
Before any stream crossing, check the gauge reading at R-4. Do not cross if the
reading exceeds +0.40 m above baseline. The buddy system is mandatory — never
cross alone. Undo your hip belt and loosen shoulder straps before entering the
water. Use a stout pole on the upstream side for stability and cross only at
designated fords [1]. After heavy rain (>15 mm in 12 hours), assume gauge is at
Caution level even without a direct reading. Wait at least 6 hours after
precipitation peak before crossing [2].
